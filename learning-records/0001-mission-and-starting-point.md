# Mission set; entry point chosen at the homotopy interpretation

Session 1 established the mission (see [[MISSION.md]]): a mathematically mature learner
(strong topology / algebra / category theory) wants to understand HoTT **as a foundation** —
"types as spaces," univalence, and isomorphism-invariance — with a balanced concept/practice
mix, anchored on the HoTT Book.

Because the learner already owns spaces, paths, and groupoids, the course opens directly at
the **homotopy interpretation** rather than at raw MLTT syntax. Lesson 1 teaches the
type↔space dictionary and **proof-relevance** (identity types can have many inhabitants).

**Implications for next sessions:** assume homotopy/category-theory fluency; motivate syntax
with pictures, then formalize. The natural next floor is **path induction (J)** — the engine
behind all higher-groupoid structure — provided L1's proof-relevance idea lands. Confirm that
landing (e.g. the learner can say why `refl` being the sole constructor coexists with many
paths) before advancing.

**Evidence:** none yet — mission stated via intake questions; understanding not yet
demonstrated. Update once the learner engages with L1's quiz or asks a diagnostic follow-up.
